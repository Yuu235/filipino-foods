How to Make Lumpia

Lumpia

Lumpia is a popular Filipino spring roll that is often filled with a mixture of vegetables and meat. It is typically served with a sweet and sour dipping sauce.

There are many variations of lumpia, including fresh and fried versions. It is a popular appetizer or snack in Filipino cuisine. It is perfect for parties or gatherings and is sure to be a hit with your family and friends.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound ground pork
  • 1 cup shredded carrots
  • 1 cup chopped cabbage
  • 1/2 cup chopped green onions
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 package lumpia wrappers
  • Oil for frying

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine ground pork, carrots, cabbage, green onions, garlic, soy sauce, and oyster sauce. Mix well until all ingredients are evenly combined.
  2. Take a lumpia wrapper and place about 2 tablespoons of the filling on one end.
  3. Fold the sides over the filling and roll tightly. Seal the edge with a little water.
  4. Repeat until all filling is used.
  5. Heat oil in a deep pan over medium heat.
  6. Fry the lumpia until golden brown and crispy.
  7. Drain on paper towels before serving.

Enjoy your homemade lumpia!
