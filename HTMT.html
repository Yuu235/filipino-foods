How to Make Tocino

Tocino?

Tocino is a sweet cured pork dish that is popular in the Philippines. It is often served with garlic rice and a fried egg, making it a delicious breakfast option.

It is typically made with pork belly or shoulder, marinated in a mixture of sugar, salt, and spices, and then cooked until caramelized.

Tocino is known for its sweet and savory flavor, and it is a favorite among Filipinos.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg pork (preferably shoulder or belly), sliced thin
  • 1 tbsp salt
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tbsp garlic powder
  • ½ cup sugar
  • ¼ cup pineapple juice
  • 2 tbsp vinegar
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • Red food coloring (optional)
  • 1 tbsp oil (for cooking)

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, mix salt, pepper, garlic powder, sugar, pineapple juice, vinegar, soy sauce, and red food coloring (if using).
  2. Add sliced pork and mix until well coated.
  3. Cover and refrigerate to marinate for at least 24 hours.
  4. To cook, heat oil in a pan over medium heat.
  5. Add marinated pork and a bit of the marinade.
  6. Cook until the sauce thickens and the pork becomes caramelized and slightly charred.
  7. Serve with garlic rice and a fried egg for a classic "Tosilog" meal!

Enjoy your homemade tocino!
