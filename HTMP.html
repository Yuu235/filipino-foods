How to Make Pancit

Pancit

Pancit is a traditional Filipino noodle dish that comes in various forms, often stir-fried with vegetables and meat. It is a popular dish for celebrations and gatherings, symbolizing long life and good fortune.

Pancit is often served with calamansi, a small citrus fruit, for added flavor.

There are many regional variations of pancit, each with its own unique ingredients and flavors.

Ingredients

  • 1 pack bihon or canton noodles
  • 2 cups chicken or pork, sliced
  • 1 cup carrots, julienned
  • 1 cup cabbage, shredded
  • 1 cup green beans, sliced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 cups chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons oyster sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Soak bihon in warm water until soft (skip for canton).
  2. Heat oil and saut√© garlic and onions.
  3. Add meat and cook until browned.
  4. Pour in broth, soy sauce, and oyster sauce.
  5. Bring to a boil.
  6. Add vegetables and cook for 3 minutes.
  7. Add noodles and mix until liquid is absorbed.
  8. Stir-fry for 5 minutes until fully cooked.
  9. Serve with calamansi.
